Zoonotic Outbreak

A zoonotic outbreak occurs when a pathogen (virus, bacterium, parasite, or fungus) that usually infects animals crosses the species barrier and begins infecting humans, sometimes spreading person-to-person afterward. This simulation models that spread to predict outbreak hotspots.

Types of Pathogens Involved

  • Viruses: e.g., SARS-CoV, MERS-CoV, Ebola virus, Influenza (H1N1, H5N1), Nipah virus, COVID-19 (SARS-CoV-2)
  • Bacteria: e.g., Salmonella, Brucella, Yersinia pestis (plague), Leptospira
  • Parasites: e.g., Toxoplasma gondii, Trypanosoma species
  • Fungi: e.g., Histoplasma capsulatum (found in bat and bird droppings)

Common Animal Sources

  • Wildlife: bats, rodents, primates, birds
  • Livestock: cattle, pigs, camels, poultry, goats
  • Pets: cats, dogs, reptiles

Transmission Pathways

  • Direct contact: bites, scratches, bodily fluids
  • Indirect contact: contaminated food, water, or surfaces
  • Vector-borne: mosquitoes, ticks, fleas
  • Airborne: respiratory droplets or aerosols
  • Foodborne: undercooked or contaminated animal products
